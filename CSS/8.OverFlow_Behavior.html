2. Overflow: Hidden

India is highly susceptible to earthquakes because it lies on the boundary of the Indian Plate and the Eurasian Plate. Northern India, especially states like Jammu & Kashmir, Himachal Pradesh, and Uttarakhand, experiences frequent seismic activity due to the Himalayan collision zone. T he eastern and northeastern states, including Assam and Sikkim, are also highly vulnerable. Earthquakes in India have historically caused massive damage to infrastructure and loss of life, highlighting the need for strong disaster management systems.

3. Overflow: Scroll

Pakistan is situated in a seismically active region because of the collision between the Indian Plate and the Eurasian Plate. Areas such as Balochistan and northern Pakistan, including the regions near Islamabad and Kashmir, are especially prone to earthquakes. Major earthquakes in Pakistan's history, like the 2005 Kashmir earthquake, have caused thousands of deaths and extensive destruction. Urban planning and infrastructure development are critical in reducing the risks associated with seismic events.

4. Overflow: Auto

Japan is one of the most earthquake-prone countries in the world due to its location on the Pacific Ring of Fire, where multiple tectonic plates meet. The country experiences frequent tremors, from minor earthquakes to massive ones like the 2011 Tohoku earthquake and tsunami. Japan has developed advanced earthquake-resistant infrastructure, early warning systems, and strict building codes to minimize casualties and damage. The nation serves as a global example of how preparedness and technology can reduce the impact of natural disasters.
